Advances in molecular medicine have transformed cancer treatment. Genetic research enables the development of more effective personalized therapies. Clinical trials evaluate the safety and efficacy of new drugs. Medical protocols establish guidelines for the diagnosis and treatment of disease. 

Human anatomy comprises interconnected systems and organs. Physiology studies the normal functions of the human body. Pathologies alter the normal functioning of tissues and organs. Symptoms help physicians identify possible diseases. 

Vaccines have been instrumental in the prevention of infectious diseases. Immunology studies the body's defense mechanisms. Antibodies protect against specific pathogens through immune responses. Microbiology investigates bacteria, viruses and other pathogenic microorganisms. 

Surgery requires precise techniques and in-depth anatomical knowledge. Anesthesia allows painless surgical procedures. Surgical instruments must be kept in sterile conditions. Postoperative rehabilitation is crucial for the patient's recovery.
